His Early Life

Albert Einstein was born in Germany in 1789 to Hermann and Pauline. He grew up in a secular Jewish family. He grew up with one sister, named Maja. He attended elementary school in Munich. He also had speech challenges. He then had begun developing a passion for classical music and violin playing. When he was a little bit older he had a tutor who introduced him to new sciences that inspired Einstein. As a result, he wrote his first major paper in his teen years about the magnetic fields.
